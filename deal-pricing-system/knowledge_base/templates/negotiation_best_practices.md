# Deal Negotiation Best Practices

## 3-Step Strategic Framework
1. **Preparation**: Pre-work, demand plan, customer scoping
2. **Analysis & Strategy**: Proposal scenarios, concession planning, pricing terms
3. **Delivery**: Present, negotiate, close

## Demand Plan Categories
- **Validated**: Budgeted, has project owner/schedule — include in proposals
- **Projected**: Credible interest, may not be budgeted — include in proposals
- **Pipeline**: Limited exposure, unconsidered needs — do NOT include in proposals, use seeding bundles

## Proposal Best Practices
- **2 scenarios is better than 3**
  - Scenario 1: Customer's request (volumes, timing, product mix)
  - Scenario 2: Backbase recommendation leveraging financial levers
- **3rd scenario ONLY if**: negotiation stuck on unrealistic expectations, or to show economics of a single lever
- **5-year time horizon**: Position shorter terms as a concession
- **Breakeven target: 12-16 months** — makes it appealing to CFOs

## Concession Strategy (Martini — Best Practice)
Round 1 (Opening): **Zero concession** — "I'm serious about my first offer"
Round 2 (Counter): **Largest concession** — address customer risks, pace concessions
Round 3 (Closing): **Small, superficial** — signal you are done, remain measured

Avoid: Wrecking Ball (one large concession), Avalanche (larger each round), Lollipop (all concession upfront)

## Common Concession Levers
| Category | Lever | Description |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| Pricing | Volume discounts | Based on usage or commitment |
| Pricing | Payment terms | Extended net-30 or net-60 |
| Pricing | Pricing tiers | Based on commitment and growth |
| Pricing | Renewal cap | Reduce cap or flat renewal |
| Pricing | VPA/Price holds | Pre-negotiated discounts on future purchases |
| Legal | Contract term | 3, 5, 10 years |
| Legal | Swap language | Re-allocate unused licenses (limit 3x/year) |
| Marketing | Case study | External-facing joint success material |
| Marketing | Press release | Co-sponsored external |
| Product | Advisory board | Client focus group for product guidance |
| Product | SLAs | Support tiers, downtime thresholds |
| Sales Ops | License activation | Buy up-front, staggered, or price ramp |
| Sales Ops | Stub billing | Break annual payment into smaller parts |

## Give-to-Get Requirements
Concessions require customer agreement on:
- **Timing** of commitment
- **Current agreements** (avoid attrition)
- **Inclusions & exclusions** (product, segments, user types)
- **Contract term** (lead with X years, concede to Y years)
- **Funding commitment** & obstacles to execute

## Proposal Storyline Structure
1. **Our Journey / Mutual Benefits** — anchor to what matters most to customer
2. **Pricing Options** — 2 scenarios
3. **Concession History** — remind client of each concession provided
4. **Business Case** — justify the investment (breakeven, ROI)
5. **Case Studies** — reasons to believe
6. **Mutual Close Plan** — agreed steps to complete the deal

## Delivery Best Practices
- Plan a **final exec give** (get approvals before you need it)
- **Capped renewals**: Don't give away 0% uplift — make it a give-to-get
- **Ask for the business** once trust is earned
- **Power of silence** — extended silence leads to better outcomes
- **"No" is acceptable** at closing stage
