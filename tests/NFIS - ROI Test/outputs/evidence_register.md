## PART 4: KEY STRATEGIC THEMES

1. **Massive Activation Gap**: The single largest value leak is the 50% of accounts that close without ever being funded, plus 25% of open accounts remaining unfunded. Fixing the onboarding-to-funding journey is the highest-ROI intervention.

2. **Awareness is the Top-of-Funnel Bottleneck**: With only ~115K of potentially millions of NFCU members using DI, and discovery being 100% organic through the mobile app link, there is enormous untapped demand -- especially given that "building wealth" is consistently the #3 goal cited by new members.

3. **Email-Only Engagement**: The complete absence of in-app push notifications, SMS, and real-time nudging across the banking app is a critical gap. All engagement is email-based and largely manual.

4. **Island Architecture**: Digital Investor operates as a standalone island -- separate app, separate contact center, separate goals system, no visibility in the banking app, no cross-product triggers. This is the root cause of multiple pain points.

5. **Servicing is Heavily Manual**: Physical forms, manual data pulls, email-based workflows across 3+ systems, and a small support team create high cost-to-serve and poor member experience. The 50% document rejection rate alone signals massive process inefficiency.

6. **Asset Retention at Risk**: Funds are flowing to Schwab, Vanguard, Fidelity, and Robinhood. A predictive model (TWB) exists but is not yet connected to the DI platform for proactive retention.

7. **Tier Transition is Nonexistent**: There is no automated path from DI to advisor as members accumulate wealth. The threshold (mentioned as $2K to $50K for IA, $50K+ for FA) is not system-detected.

8. **Custodian Migration (to Apex)**: An upcoming migration to Apex is expected to unlock new capabilities (limit orders, better event-driven processing, ACAT improvements) -- a key dependency for several improvements.
