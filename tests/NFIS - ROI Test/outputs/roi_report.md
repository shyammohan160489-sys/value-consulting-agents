## Structural Observations

### Why NFIS Per-Member Economics Are Low

NFIS operates under a credit union "member-first" philosophy (E43) with deliberately low fees:
- Robo: 0.275% on avg $4,350 AUM = $11.96/yr
- Self-Directed: $3.50/mo = $42/yr
- Blended DI revenue per member: ~$22-32/yr

For comparison, wealth management clients generate $1,700-5,400/yr (Goodbody segments in wealth/benchmarks.md). This 50-200x revenue differential explains why an investing ROI is structurally harder to achieve than wealth/retail banking ROI.

**The value creation path for NFIS is volume, not yield.** The 14M member base is the asset. Moving from <1% to even 2% penetration doubles the DI business. The Backbase value proposition is primarily about **access** (making DI visible and frictionless for the banking base) rather than **optimization** (improving fee yields on existing accounts).

### Why L7 Retention Applies to $7.4B AUA

The lever candidates document retention as "preventing asset outflow to competitors" — this applies to all NFIS assets, not just DI. The unified banking + investing experience creates stickiness across tiers. The TWB (Transition to Wealth Building) predictive model (E12) covers the full member base. Applying retention only to $500M DI AUM would undercount the benefit of a unified digital platform that serves both DI and FA members.
