NatWest partnered with OpenAI for enterprise AI — but they're a Tier 1 bank with 60,000+ staff and a £1.2B+ annual tech budget. Schroders Wealth Management has 225 RMs. The economics are fundamentally different. NatWest isn't even self-hosting — they're using API access at massive scale.

THE CORE PROBLEM — VOLUME
Schroders' 10 agents consume ~£13K/yr in raw compute. Self-hosting breaks even at ~£500K+ API spend. You're 40× below the threshold. Self-hosting means paying £700K+ to save £13K.
5 REASONS TO STAY ON BIC
1. Cost: 2-3× more expensive at your volume (~1M BICs/yr)
2. Model flexibility: BIC = multi-model (GPT, Claude, Gemini, Llama). Self-host = locked to ONE
3. Speed: BIC = live in weeks. Self-hosted = 6-12 months build-out
4. Talent: MLOps specialists at £120-150K each — hard to hire, harder to retain
5. Maintenance: Model deprecation, security patches, fine-tuning, compliance auditing — all yours
WHEN SELF-HOSTING MAKES SENSE (FUTURE)
→ Regulatory mandate for data residency (no cloud AI)
→ Volume grows to 10M+ BICs (50× current — unlikely with 225 RMs)
→ Group-wide AI programme with dedicated MLOps team
→ If you get there, Backbase can support self-hosted infrastructure
The door stays open — but the economics don't justify walking through it today.
